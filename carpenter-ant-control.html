Common Signs You Need Carpenter Ant Control

Carpenter ants often remain hidden until the infestation becomes established. Common signs include:

  • Large black ants inside the home
  • Fine wood shavings (frass) beneath walls or wood trim
  • Rustling sounds inside walls or ceilings
  • Winged carpenter ants during spring
  • Damaged or hollow sounding wood

Where Carpenter Ants Commonly Nest

Carpenter ants prefer damp or damaged wood and may establish nests in:

Crawl spaces Wall voids Attics Decks Wooden siding Window and door frames Roof structures Moist structural wood

Our Carpenter Ant Control Process

1 Inspection

2 Treatment Recommendations

3 Targeted Treatment

4 Prevention Guidance

Why Professional Carpenter Ant Control Matters

Unlike many other ants, carpenter ants excavate wood to create nesting galleries. Eliminating visible ants alone may not resolve the problem if the colony remains active. Professional inspections help locate nesting sites and determine the most appropriate treatment approach.
