Wasp Facts Homeowners Should Know

Wasps are active across Coeur d Alene more than most people think. They love water, trees and wood siding. Our informative staff can walk you through what to look for so you can spot a problem early.

How Wasp Colonies Grow

A wasp colony can grow from just a few wasps to over a hundred in one season. The nest starts small in spring but grows fast once more workers join in. This is why a small nest in April can turn into a real problem by July.

What Attracts Wasps to a Backyard

Wasps look for shelter, food and easy nesting spots. Shaded eaves, wood fences and garden sheds all attract them. Sweet food and nectar from flowers also draw wasps into a yard. Trash cans left open are another common draw.

Why Wasps Get More Aggressive in Late Summer

Early in the season wasps stay busy building the nest. They are usually calm unless disturbed. By late summer the colony reaches full size and food gets harder to find. Wasps become more defensive during this time. This is why most calls for help come in during August and September.

Wasp Identification near CDA Areas

Not every stinging insect is the same. Our knowledgeable staff can walk you through why treatment methods vary once we know what we are dealing with.

Paper Wasp

Builds small open nests shaped like an upside down umbrella usually under eaves or porch ceilings. Generally calm unless the nest is disturbed.

Yellowjacket

Builds nests in the ground, old rodent burrows or inside wall voids. Defends the nest aggressively and can sting more than once.

Bald Faced Hornet

Builds large enclosed paper nests in trees or on the sides of buildings. Defensive by nature and can attack in groups if the nest is threatened.

Not sure which one is in your yard? Our helpful team identifies it during the inspection so treatment matches the exact species.
