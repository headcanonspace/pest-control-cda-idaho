Common Signs You Need Flea Control

Fleas often remain unnoticed until the infestation begins to spread. Common signs include:

  • Pets scratching or biting excessively
  • Small jumping insects on carpets or furniture
  • Flea bites around ankles or lower legs
  • Dark flea dirt on pet bedding
  • Fleas appearing in multiple rooms

Where Fleas Commonly Hide

Fleas often develop in warm, protected areas where pets or wildlife spend time, including:

Carpets and rugs Pet bedding Upholstered furniture Floor cracks Baseboards Crawl spaces Garages Shaded outdoor areas

Our Flea Control Process

Every infestation is different, so treatment recommendations begin with understanding where fleas are living and how widespread the problem has become.

1 Inspection

Identify flea hot spots, breeding areas, and conditions that support flea infestations.

2 Treatment Recommendations

Recommendations are based on the property's layout, the affected areas, and the extent of the infestation.

3 Targeted Treatment

Treatment focuses on areas where fleas are most likely to hide, breed, and develop.

4 Prevention Guidance

Recommendations may include vacuuming, washing pet bedding, treating pets as recommended by a veterinarian, and reducing conditions that encourage flea populations.

Why Professional Flea Control Matters

Fleas reproduce quickly, and treating only visible adults may not eliminate the infestation. Professional flea control focuses on disrupting the flea life cycle while identifying areas where fleas are breeding to help reduce future problems.
