How Pests Behave in Coeur d'Alene (Seasonal Calendar & Risks)

Get to it at the right point in the season and it's a quick fix, wait too long and it turns into a much bigger job.

March-April (Spring Thaw)

When spring shows up and ants start moving again, they're usually looking for whatever gap opened up in your foundation over the winter. Give it a few days from that first scout, and you've usually got a full trail heading straight into the kitchen. That's the best period to knock it out early, before it's more than just a few ants wandering in.

May-August (Peak Season)

Wasps and yellow jackets start small in spring, but by summer, that nest's grown a lot, sometimes into the hundreds by August. And mosquitoes, they don't need much, just some water sitting in a gutter or under a sprinkler for about a week and you've got a breeding spot. Easiest time to deal with any of this is early, before the nest's big enough to get defensive about it.

September-October (First Cooldown)

As soon as it starts cooling off, spiders head indoors looking for somewhere warm, usually through a garage gap or a crawl space vent that was never quite sealed. Rodents are doing the same thing around this time, and once one finds a way in, it doesn't take long before there's a bigger opening for the next one. This is usually when people start noticing pests inside even though nothing outside really changed.

November-February (Winter Shelter)

Once it's cold enough, most insects go quiet, but rodents stick around all winter, tucked into attics and wall spaces where it's warm. The damage they do while they're in there, chewing, nesting, usually doesn't show up until spring, since it's happening somewhere you're not checking during the winter.
