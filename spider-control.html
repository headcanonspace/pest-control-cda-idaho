Common Signs You Need Spider Control

Spider problems may become more noticeable as populations increase indoors. Common signs include:

  • Spider webs around windows, ceilings, and corners
  • Egg sacs attached to walls or outdoor structures
  • Frequent spider sightings indoors
  • Spiders in garages, basements, or crawl spaces
  • Increased insect populations attracting spiders

Our Spider Control Process

Every property presents different conditions, so recommendations begin with understanding the problem.

1 Inspection

Identify spider species, web locations, entry points, and areas where spiders are commonly found.

2 Treatment Recommendations

Recommendations are based on the spider involved, the property's layout, and the affected areas.

3 Targeted Treatment

Treatment focuses on common hiding places, web locations, and entry points where spiders are most likely to remain.

4 Prevention Guidance

Recommendations may include reducing clutter, sealing entry points, limiting outdoor lighting that attracts insects, and removing conditions that encourage spiders.

Why Professional Spider Control Matters

Many spiders remain hidden in areas that are rarely disturbed. Simply removing visible webs may not address the conditions that allow spiders to return. Professional inspections help identify common hiding places, entry points, and factors that contribute to recurring spider problems.

Important Information About Hobo Spiders

Hobo spiders are commonly found in parts of the Pacific Northwest, including North Idaho. They are sometimes mistaken for brown recluse spiders, although brown recluse spiders are not native to Idaho. Proper identification helps determine the most appropriate treatment recommendations.
