Common Signs You Need Rodent Control

Rodents often remain hidden until the infestation becomes established. Common signs include:

  • Mouse or rat droppings
  • Gnaw marks on wood, food packaging, or wiring
  • Scratching sounds in walls or ceilings
  • Nesting materials such as shredded paper or insulation
  • Unpleasant odors in enclosed areas

Our Rodent Control Process

Every rodent infestation is different, so recommendations begin with identifying how rodents are entering the property and where they are nesting.

1 Inspection

Inspect the property for rodent entry points, nesting locations, droppings, and signs of damage.

2 Treatment Recommendations

Recommendations are based on the rodent involved, the property's layout, and the affected areas.

3 Targeted Treatment

Treatment focuses on active nesting locations, travel routes, and common entry points.

4 Prevention Guidance

Recommendations may include sealing entry points, improving sanitation, reducing food and water sources, and removing conditions that encourage rodents.

Why Professional Rodent Control Matters

Rodents can contaminate food, damage insulation, gnaw electrical wiring, and spread throughout a property if left untreated. Professional rodent control focuses on identifying entry points, locating nesting areas, and recommending practical prevention measures to help reduce recurring infestations.

Deer Mice and Hantavirus Information

Deer mice are found throughout parts of Idaho and can carry Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS). If you discover rodent droppings or nesting materials inside a cabin, garage, shed, or other enclosed structure, avoid sweeping or vacuuming the area, as this may disturb contaminated dust. Follow appropriate cleaning precautions and consider having the property inspected before cleanup.
